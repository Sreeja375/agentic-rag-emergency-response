FIRST AID INFORMATION

1. Severe Bleeding
- Apply direct pressure using a clean cloth or bandage.
- Elevate the injured part above heart level if possible.
- Do not remove deeply embedded objects.
- Seek emergency medical help immediately.

2. Burns
- Cool the burn under running water for at least 10 minutes.
- Do not apply ice, butter, or oils.
- Cover the burn loosely with a clean cloth.
- Seek medical help for severe burns.

3. Fractures
- Do not move the injured limb unnecessarily.
- Immobilize the area using a splint if available.
- Apply a cold pack to reduce swelling.
- Take the person to a hospital immediately.

4. Unconscious Person
- Check if the person is breathing.
- If breathing, place them in the recovery position.
- Do not give food or water.
- Call emergency services immediately.

5. Choking
- Encourage coughing if the person can breathe.
- If unable to breathe, perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver).
- Call emergency services if choking continues.

6. Heart Attack
- Help the person sit and rest.
- Loosen tight clothing.
- If prescribed, assist with medication.
- Call emergency services immediately.

NOTE:
This information is for first-aid guidance only and does not replace professional medical care.
